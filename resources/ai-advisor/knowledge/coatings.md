# Lens Coatings

## Anti-Reflective Coating (AR)

Anti-reflective coating reduces glare and reflections from both the front and back surfaces of the lens. Benefits include:

- Clearer, sharper vision — especially in low light and at night
- Reduced eye strain during extended screen use
- Lenses that appear nearly invisible, so others see your eyes rather than a reflection
- Better performance from headlights and artificial lighting while driving at night

AR coating is recommended for nearly all lens types and is especially useful with high-index lenses, which have more internal reflectivity than standard plastic.

Most AR coatings at 39DollarGlasses also include scratch resistance and UV protection as part of the coating package.

## Blue-Light Filtering

Blue-light filtering lenses are designed to reduce exposure to high-energy visible (HEV) blue light emitted by digital screens, LED lighting, and other artificial light sources.

Many people who use digital devices for extended periods report symptoms like eye fatigue, difficulty focusing, and disrupted sleep. Blue-light filtering lenses are designed to address these experiences, though results vary by individual.

39DollarGlasses offers blue-light filtering options at different levels:

- **Standard blue-light filter** — a mild filter included in many AR coating options; reduces some blue light without affecting color perception significantly.
- **Blue495** — a more targeted filter designed to block blue light up to the 495nm wavelength. See **blue495.md** for details.

Blue-light lenses are not a medical treatment and are not a substitute for advice from an eye care professional.

## UV Protection

UV protection blocks ultraviolet light from reaching your eyes. Most lens materials offered by 39DollarGlasses include UV protection, and it is also included in AR and other coating packages.

UV protection is a standard feature and does not affect lens appearance or clarity.

## Scratch-Resistant Coating

Scratch-resistant coating adds a harder surface layer to help lenses resist everyday scratches. It does not make lenses completely scratchproof, but significantly reduces wear from routine use.

Most coating packages at 39DollarGlasses include scratch resistance.

## Tint

Lenses can be tinted in a range of colors for fashion, light comfort, or sun protection. Common options include:

- **Gray tint** — reduces brightness without distorting color
- **Brown/amber tint** — warms color perception, can enhance contrast
- **Green tint** — natural color balance with good contrast
- **Fashion tints** — available in a variety of colors for aesthetic purposes

Tinted lenses for outdoor use should ideally include UV protection. A darker tint does not automatically mean more UV protection — check that UV protection is included.

## Polarized Coating

Polarized lenses reduce glare from reflective surfaces like water, roads, and snow. They are popular for driving, fishing, and outdoor activities.

Polarized lenses are available as a sunglass option. See **sunglass-lenses.md** for more detail.

## Photochromic (Transitions)

Photochromic lenses darken automatically in sunlight and return to clear indoors. They combine everyday clear lenses with sunglass convenience in one pair. See **transitions.md** for details.
