# Transitions (Photochromic) Lenses

## What Are Photochromic Lenses?

Photochromic lenses — often called Transitions lenses, after the leading brand — darken automatically when exposed to UV light outdoors and return to a clear (or nearly clear) state indoors. They are designed to serve as both everyday clear lenses and sunglasses in a single pair.

## How They Work

Photochromic lenses contain light-sensitive molecules embedded in the lens material. When ultraviolet light from sunlight activates these molecules, the lens darkens. When UV exposure is reduced — indoors, in a car with UV-blocking glass, or after sunset — the molecules relax and the lens clears.

The transition speed varies by temperature. Lenses typically darken faster and reach their darkest state in cooler conditions. In warm weather, they may not darken as deeply or clear as quickly.

## Key Limitations to Know

- **Behind a windshield**: Modern car windshields are designed to block UV light, which means photochromic lenses may not darken significantly while driving. If driving sun protection is a primary goal, dedicated polarized sunglasses may be a better fit.
- **Temperature effect**: In hot summer conditions, lenses may not get as dark as they would in cooler weather. In cold conditions, they may take longer to clear after coming indoors.
- **Not fully clear indoors**: Photochromic lenses have a slight base tint even in their clearest state. This is minor but can be noticeable to some wearers.

## Who Photochromic Lenses Work Well For

- People who move frequently between indoor and outdoor environments
- People who do not want to carry a separate pair of sunglasses
- People whose outdoor activities involve moderate to bright sunlight
- Prescription wearers looking for convenience in a single pair

## Who Should Consider Dedicated Sunglasses Instead

- People who drive frequently in bright sunlight (due to windshield UV blocking)
- People who need dark lens coverage in very hot conditions
- People with very specific sun glare needs, such as fishing or water sports (where polarized lenses may be preferred)

## Available Colors

Photochromic lenses are available in gray and brown as common base tints. Gray maintains natural color perception when darkened; brown warms color slightly and can enhance contrast in certain conditions.

## Photochromic and Coatings

Anti-reflective coating works well with photochromic lenses and is recommended to improve clarity when the lenses are in their clear indoor state.
