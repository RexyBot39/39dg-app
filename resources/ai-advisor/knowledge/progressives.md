# Progressive Lenses

## What Are Progressives?

Progressive lenses allow you to see clearly at multiple distances — far, intermediate, and near — through a single lens with no visible line. The lens gradually transitions between focal zones from top to bottom.

- **Top of the lens**: distance vision (driving, watching TV, walking)
- **Middle of the lens**: intermediate vision (computer, dashboard, shelves)
- **Bottom of the lens**: near vision (reading, phone, close-up tasks)

Progressives are the modern alternative to bifocals and trifocals. Most people prefer them because there is no visible line and the transition between zones is smooth.

## Who Needs Progressives?

Progressives are typically prescribed for people who have difficulty seeing clearly at both near and far distances. This often begins in the mid-40s as the eye's natural lens becomes less flexible — a condition called presbyopia.

If your prescription includes an "Add" power (sometimes written as "ADD" or "+Add"), you likely need multifocal correction such as progressives or bifocals.

## Adjustment Period

Most new progressive wearers need an adjustment period of a few days to a few weeks. During this time, you may notice:

- Peripheral softness or slight distortion at the edges of the lens
- A need to position your head slightly differently when switching between focal zones
- Mild depth-perception adjustment when walking or using stairs

These effects typically diminish as your brain adapts to the new lens. If discomfort persists beyond a few weeks, consult your eye care professional.

## Choosing Frames for Progressives

Progressive lenses require sufficient vertical height in the lens area to accommodate all three focal zones. As a general guideline:

- **Minimum 28mm of vertical lens height** is recommended for most progressive designs
- **30mm or more** is preferred for the best reading zone and viewing comfort
- **Larger, taller frames** tend to work better than small or very narrow frames
- Avoid very small, narrow, or heavily angled frames that compress the lens area

See **strong-prescription-frame-guide.md** for frame guidance if you also have a strong prescription.
