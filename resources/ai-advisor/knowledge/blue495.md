# Blue495 Lenses

## What Is Blue495?

Blue495 is 39DollarGlasses' targeted blue-light filtering lens option. Unlike standard blue-light filters, which reduce only a portion of high-energy visible light, Blue495 is designed to block blue light across the spectrum up to 495 nanometers.

The 495nm threshold is a commonly referenced point in blue-light filtering because it captures the range of wavelengths most associated with digital eye strain and light-related disruptions to evening alertness.

## Who Is Blue495 For?

Blue495 may be worth considering for:

- People who spend many hours each day in front of digital screens
- People who work late on computers or devices and want to reduce blue-light exposure in the evening hours
- People who experience eye fatigue or discomfort during extended screen use and want to try a more aggressive blue-light filtering option than standard AR coating alone

## How Blue495 Differs from Standard Blue-Light Coating

| Feature | Standard Blue-Light Filter | Blue495 |
|---|---|---|
| Filtering range | Partial blue-light reduction | Up to 495nm |
| Lens appearance | Clear to very slight tint | May have a slight amber or yellow cast depending on lens and lighting |
| Best for | General screen use | Extended screen use, evening light sensitivity |

The slight tint in Blue495 lenses is a natural result of the filtering material and varies depending on lighting conditions.

## What Blue495 Is Not

Blue495 is a light-filtering lens product. It is not a medical treatment and is not intended to diagnose, treat, or cure any eye condition, sleep disorder, or medical issue. Results vary by individual.

If you have concerns about light sensitivity, sleep disruption, or eye health, please consult an eye care professional.

## Pairing Blue495 with Other Options

Blue495 can be added to single vision lenses, progressive lenses, and other standard lens types. It pairs well with anti-reflective coating for general screen use and can also be considered alongside OcuSleep lenses for people specifically focused on evening blue-light exposure management.
