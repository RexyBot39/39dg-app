# Lens Replacement

## What Is Lens Replacement?

Lens replacement is the service of putting new lenses into a frame you already own. Instead of buying a complete pair of glasses, you send in your existing frame and receive it back with new prescription or non-prescription lenses installed.

## When Lens Replacement Makes Sense

Lens replacement is a practical option when:

- Your prescription has changed but your frames are in good condition and you want to keep them
- You purchased a frame elsewhere (from a designer, a boutique, or a previous optician) and want prescription lenses made for it
- You have a favorite pair of sunglass frames and want prescription sunglass lenses installed
- Your current lenses are scratched or worn but your frames are fine
- You want to add a coating (like anti-reflective) to lenses in a frame you already love

## How It Works

The general process for lens replacement is:

1. You send your frames to 39DollarGlasses.
2. Your existing lenses are removed carefully.
3. New lenses are cut and fitted to your frame.
4. Your frame is returned with the new lenses installed.

Specific instructions, shipping details, and turnaround time are available on the lens replacement page of the 39DollarGlasses website or through customer support.

## Frame Compatibility

Most standard full-rim frames are suitable for lens replacement. Frames in good structural condition tend to work best — frames that are bent, cracked, or have broken hinges may not hold new lenses properly.

Rimless and semi-rimless frames can often be done as well, though lens replacement on these styles requires more precision. Contact our support team if you have a question about a specific frame style.

## Lens Options for Replacement

All standard lens types and coatings available for new orders are also available for lens replacement:

- Single vision, reading, progressive
- Standard plastic, polycarbonate, or high-index materials
- Anti-reflective coating, blue-light filtering, Blue495
- Tinted or polarized sunglass lenses
- Specialty lenses (Neurolux, OcuSleep, and others — confirm availability with support)
