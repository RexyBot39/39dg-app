# Frame Sizing Guide

## How Eyeglass Frames Are Measured

Eyeglass frames use a standard three-number sizing system printed on the inside of most temples (the arms that go behind your ears). The format is:

**Lens width – Bridge width – Temple length**

Example: **52-18-140**

- **52** = lens width in millimeters (horizontal width of one lens)
- **18** = bridge width in millimeters (the distance between the two lenses across the nose)
- **140** = temple length in millimeters (the arm from hinge to tip)

## Finding Your Size

The best starting point is a pair of glasses you already own and fit well. Check the inside of one of the temples for the size numbers.

If you do not have a reference pair, a general guide by face width:

| Frame Size Category | Lens Width |
|---|---|
| Narrow / Small | 48mm and under |
| Medium | 49mm – 53mm |
| Wide | 54mm – 57mm |
| Extra Wide | 58mm and above |

These are general guidelines. Bridge width and temple length also affect how a frame feels, especially for nose fit and how far behind the ear the temples extend.

## Bridge Fit

The bridge width determines how the frame sits across your nose. A bridge that is too narrow will sit high or pinch; one that is too wide will slide down.

- Most adults wear a bridge in the 16mm–20mm range
- Adjustable nose pads (common on metal frames) allow more flexibility for bridge fit
- Many plastic frames have a fixed bridge and cannot be adjusted

## Temple Length

Temple length (typically 135mm–145mm) affects how the frame wraps behind your ear. Longer temples are needed for larger heads or a more secure fit.

## Frame Size and Prescription

For stronger prescriptions, lens width matters more than just fit — narrower lenses mean less material at the edges, which keeps thick lenses thinner and lighter.

See **strong-prescription-frame-guide.md** for specific guidance on choosing frames for stronger prescriptions.

## Measuring Your Face

If you do not have a reference pair of glasses, you can measure your face with a ruler or soft tape measure:

- **Face width**: measure across your face at the widest point (typically across the cheekbones or temples)
- The frame's total width (lens width × 2 + bridge width) should be close to but not wider than your face width

Online virtual try-on tools can also be helpful for checking how a frame looks before ordering.

## Kids' Frames

Children generally need smaller frames with shorter temples. A comfortable fit for a child typically starts in the 43mm–48mm lens width range depending on age. Adjustable nose pads and flexible hinge designs can help ensure a secure fit for active children.
