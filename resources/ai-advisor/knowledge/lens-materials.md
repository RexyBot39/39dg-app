# Lens Materials

## CR-39 Standard Plastic

CR-39 is the most commonly used optical plastic. It offers good optical clarity, is easy to tint, and works well for most mild to moderate prescriptions. It is the default material for many standard single vision and reading lenses.

CR-39 lenses are heavier and thicker than polycarbonate or high-index materials at the same prescription power, which makes them less ideal for stronger prescriptions or lightweight frame goals.

## Polycarbonate

Polycarbonate is lighter and more impact-resistant than standard plastic, with built-in UV protection. It is a strong choice for active use, rimless frames, and anyone who prioritizes durability.

It is thinner than CR-39 but not as thin as high-index materials. Polycarbonate is a good mid-range option for prescriptions in the moderate range.

## High-Index Materials (1.57, 1.61, 1.67, 1.74)

High-index materials allow lenses to be made thinner and lighter at the same prescription power. The higher the index number, the thinner the lens can be.

- **1.57** is a moderate step up from standard plastic, appropriate for mild to moderate prescriptions.
- **1.61** offers a noticeable improvement in thinness for moderate to moderately strong prescriptions.
- **1.67** is recommended for stronger prescriptions where lens thickness would otherwise be a concern.
- **1.74** is the thinnest commonly available index, best suited for very strong prescriptions.

High-index lenses have more internal reflectivity than standard plastic, which is why anti-reflective coating is particularly recommended with these materials.

## Choosing the Right Material

| Prescription Strength | Suggested Material |
|---|---|
| Low (under ±2.00) | CR-39 or polycarbonate |
| Moderate (±2.00 to ±4.00) | Polycarbonate or 1.57/1.61 high-index |
| Strong (±4.00 to ±6.00) | 1.61 or 1.67 high-index |
| Very strong (over ±6.00) | 1.67 or 1.74 high-index |

These are general guidelines. The right material also depends on frame choice, cylinder values, pupillary distance, and other prescription details. An eye care professional can advise on the best fit for a specific prescription.
