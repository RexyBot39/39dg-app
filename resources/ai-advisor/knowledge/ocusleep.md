# OcuSleep Lenses

## What Are OcuSleep Lenses?

OcuSleep lenses are specialty lenses designed to reduce blue-light exposure in the evening hours. They use an amber-tinted filtering approach that targets the blue and blue-green wavelengths most associated with alertness signals in the body's natural light-response cycle.

The concept behind OcuSleep is based on the relationship between blue light exposure and the body's internal clock. Exposure to blue light in the evening — from screens, LED lighting, and other artificial sources — is associated with changes in evening alertness and sleep onset for some people.

## How OcuSleep Differs from Blue495

Both OcuSleep and Blue495 are blue-light filtering products, but they are designed for different use cases:

| | Blue495 | OcuSleep |
|---|---|---|
| Primary use case | Daytime screen use | Evening use before sleep |
| Filter approach | Up to 495nm | Broader blue/blue-green range |
| Lens appearance | Mild tint | Amber-tinted |
| Color impact | Minimal | Noticeable warm shift |

OcuSleep lenses have a visible amber tint. This warm color cast is intentional — it is a result of filtering the blue and blue-green light range more aggressively. Most people use OcuSleep lenses as a dedicated evening pair rather than an all-day lens.

## Who Might Consider OcuSleep?

OcuSleep may be worth exploring for people who:

- Use screens regularly in the hours before bed
- Are interested in reducing blue-light exposure specifically in the evening
- Have experimented with standard blue-light filters and want a more aggressive option for evening use
- Are looking for a dedicated evening lens rather than an all-day blue-light solution

## What OcuSleep Is Not

OcuSleep lenses are not a medical treatment. They are not intended to diagnose, treat, or cure any sleep disorder, medical condition, or other health issue. Individual results vary, and OcuSleep lenses are not a substitute for professional medical advice about sleep health.

If you have concerns about sleep, consult a physician or sleep specialist. If you have questions about light sensitivity or eye health, consult an eye care professional.

## Prescriptions and OcuSleep

OcuSleep lenses are available with or without prescription correction. They are commonly used as a secondary pair dedicated to evening and bedtime screen use.
