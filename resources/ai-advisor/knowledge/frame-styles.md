# Frame Styles Guide

## Common Frame Shapes

### Rectangular

Rectangular frames have straight horizontal lines and squared corners. They are one of the most versatile and widely worn shapes.

- Works well on round, oval, and heart-shaped faces
- Gives a structured, defined look
- Available in a wide range of sizes, from narrow and minimalist to wide and bold
- Good for professional, everyday, and fashion contexts

### Round

Round frames have a circular or nearly circular lens shape. They have seen a strong resurgence in popularity and work well in both classic and fashion-forward contexts.

- Works particularly well on square, angular, and oblong faces
- Softens strong facial angles
- Can be small and subtle or large and bold depending on diameter
- A good shape for minimizing edge thickness in stronger prescriptions (see **strong-prescription-frame-guide.md**)

### Oval

Oval frames are a softer variation on rectangular — slightly curved edges with a gentle, elongated shape.

- One of the most versatile shapes; works well on most face shapes
- A natural, understated look that suits a wide range of styles
- Good balance between visual structure and softness

### Cat-Eye

Cat-eye frames have an upswept outer corner that lifts at the temples. They have a classic mid-century style that remains popular.

- Most flattering on oval and heart-shaped faces
- Creates an upward visual effect that can lift facial features
- Available from subtle to dramatic sweep depending on the design

### Aviator

Aviator frames feature a teardrop-shaped lens and a thin metal double bridge. Originally designed for pilots, they have been a mainstream fashion staple for decades.

- Works well on oval, heart-shaped, and square faces
- Generally a larger, wider frame
- Associated with a relaxed, confident aesthetic

### Wayfarer

Wayfarer-style frames have a broad, trapezoidal plastic front — thicker at the top than the bottom. A classic American design popular since the 1950s.

- Works well on oval, square, and oblong faces
- Bold, assertive look
- Very widely available in a range of colors and sizes

### Rimless and Semi-Rimless

Rimless frames have no surrounding frame material — lenses are mounted directly to the bridge and temples via small screws or drilling. Semi-rimless frames have a partial rim (typically just the top or bottom).

- Very lightweight; minimal visual presence on the face
- Lenses edges are exposed — not ideal for stronger prescriptions where edge thickness would be visible
- A clean, understated, and professional look
- Require careful handling as they have fewer structural supports

## Lightweight Frames

For wearers who prioritize comfort, lightweight frames are available in:

- **Titanium** — the lightest metal option; naturally hypoallergenic
- **TR-90** — a lightweight flexible plastic, very comfortable for all-day wear
- **Rimless and semi-rimless** — minimal frame material means minimal weight

See **frame-materials.md** for more detail.

## Budget and Value Frames

39DollarGlasses is built around the value proposition that great lenses and attractive frames should not require premium retail pricing. Across the frame catalog, there are options at every price point.

When selecting a budget frame:
- Prioritize good fit over brand recognition
- Full-rim plastic frames tend to be among the most affordable while still being durable
- The lens choice (material and coatings) often matters more to optical outcome than frame cost

## Frames for Progressives

Progressive lens wearers need sufficient vertical lens height (at least 28mm, ideally 30mm+). This rules out very narrow or very short lens shapes. Good progressive frame choices include:

- Medium-to-large rectangular frames
- Oval frames with adequate height
- Wayfarer-style frames
- Avoid very small, narrow, or cat-eye shapes with very short vertical lens height

See **progressives.md** for more detail.
