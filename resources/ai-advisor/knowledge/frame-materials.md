# Frame Materials

## Acetate (Plastic)

Acetate is a premium plastic material made from plant-based cellulose. It is the most common material for plastic eyeglass frames.

**Characteristics:**
- Rich color options, patterns, and finishes — acetate is the standard for most patterned or translucent plastic frames
- Can be adjusted by an optician using heat (temples and nose area can be bent for a better fit)
- Durable and holds its shape well under normal use
- Slightly heavier than metal, though still comfortable for most wearers

**Good for:** People who prefer plastic frames, want expressive colors or patterns, or want a classic look.

## Metal (Stainless Steel, Titanium, Monel, etc.)

Metal frames offer a lightweight, refined look and are often adjustable at the nose pads and temple tips.

**Characteristics:**
- Generally lighter than acetate frames
- Adjustable nose pads allow customization for bridge fit
- Available in silver, gold, gunmetal, rose gold, and other metal finishes
- Some metal frames have spring hinges for added flexibility

**Types of metal frames:**
- **Stainless steel** — common, durable, and corrosion-resistant
- **Titanium** — very lightweight, strong, and hypoallergenic; often used in ultra-light and minimalist frames
- **Monel** — a nickel alloy; durable and flexible, commonly used in mid-range metal frames

**Good for:** People who want lightweight frames, adjustable bridge fit, or a minimalist look.

## TR-90 (Flexible Plastic)

TR-90 is a lightweight thermoplastic material that is flexible and impact-resistant. It is commonly used in sport frames and children's eyewear.

**Characteristics:**
- Very lightweight — lighter than standard acetate
- Flexible and resistant to breakage from impact or bending
- Less heat-adjustable than acetate

**Good for:** Active use, sport frames, children's glasses, or anyone who prioritizes durability and lightweight comfort.

## Mixed Materials

Many frames combine materials — for example, an acetate front with metal temples, or a metal frame with plastic temple tips. These hybrid designs can balance the aesthetic benefits of plastic with the lightness of metal.

## Choosing the Right Material

| Goal | Suggested Material |
|---|---|
| Lightweight daily wear | Metal (titanium) or TR-90 |
| Bold colors and patterns | Acetate |
| Adjustable nose fit | Metal with adjustable nose pads |
| Active use or kids | TR-90 or impact-resistant plastic |
| Minimalist or professional look | Metal |
| Classic or retro look | Acetate |

Frame material is a personal preference as much as a practical decision. Comfort depends on weight, fit, and how the frame interacts with your face shape and nose bridge.
